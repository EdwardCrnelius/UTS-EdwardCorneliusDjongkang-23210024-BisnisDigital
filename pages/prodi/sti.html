STI Program

What is STI Program?

The Information Systems and Technology (STI) study program is a combination of Information Systems (IS) and Information Technology (IT) to answer the challenges of rapid technological development and digital transformation. STI studies how to design and implement digital products (applications, websites, and designs) that meet business objectives and solve everyday life problems. STI also studies emerging technologies.

STI Vision

To become an excellent study program in the field of Information Systems and Technology, especially in software engineering, and contribute to the development of new technologies based on Christian values and technopreneur spirit.

STI Mission

  1. Organizing education and teaching so as to produce quality graduates in the field of information systems and technology that meet the needs of society and industry.
  2. Carry out research to develop quality science and technology.
  3. Establish cooperation between the Information Systems and Technology study program and related institutions and industries, nationally and internationally.
  4. Fostering the character of students by instilling Christian values, integrity, work ethic, and sense of responsibility, so that they are equipped to become successful and contributing members of society.

STI Target

  1. Achieving excellence in the implementation of education so that graduates are ready to compete globally.
  2. The implementation of effective and quality learning in the field of Information Systems and Technology, both in theory and practice.
  3. The implementation of cooperation with various agencies or institutions and industries that are relevant to the expertise of study programs in the country and abroad.
  4. The formation of the character of the academic community who are able to apply work ethic and Christian values in practice in the world of work.

STI Graduated Profile

  1. Graduates are able to act and judge professionally.
  2. Graduates are able to think logically, critically and systematically in utilizing informatics/computer science knowledge to solve real problems.
  3. Graduates have the ability to analyze, design, create, and evaluate information systems that are aligned with organizational goals. Graduates are able to develop multi-platform based technology products.
  4. Graduates are able to apply knowledge in the Programming and Software Development function area in their profession.
